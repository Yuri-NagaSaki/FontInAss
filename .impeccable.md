## Design Context

### Users
FontInAss serves subtitle makers, anime fans, release-group members, and self-hosted operators who need fast, reliable subtitle font subsetting. They use the app to process ASS/SSA/SRT files, manage large local font libraries, share finished subtitle archives, and leave feedback or requests in the comments area.

### Brand Personality
Warm, precise, community-minded. The interface should feel trustworthy enough for batch production work, but soft and welcoming enough for an anime subtitle community. It should evoke calm confidence, craft, and a small sense of delight.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a Sakura editorial direction: refined Japanese stationery, soft pink-tinted neutrals, airy asymmetry, careful typography, and subtle paper-like depth. The comments area should feel like a polished community guestbook rather than a generic embedded widget. Avoid generic dashboard cards, harsh developer-console aesthetics, neon gradients, and heavy glassmorphism.

### Design Principles
1. Make technical workflows feel gentle without hiding precision.
2. Favor editorial rhythm: varied spacing, readable measure, and intentional asymmetry.
3. Keep Sakura color expressive but restrained; use sky/mint/amber only as purposeful accents.
4. Make loaded and loading states feel instant, graceful, and stable.
5. Preserve accessibility through strong focus states, readable contrast, reduced visual clutter, and motion that communicates state rather than decorates.
